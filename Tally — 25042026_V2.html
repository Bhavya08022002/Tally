Sanity check · cumulative investment vs FY30 revenue

Year-1 product build investment of ₹95–150 Cr (Base ₹120 Cr) plus three years of run cost (~₹70–90 Cr/yr) gives cumulative 4-year build-period investment of ₹400–500 Cr. Plus the parallel sales/success/channel ramp (~₹50–80 Cr/yr ramping to ₹120–160 Cr by FY30) adds ~₹250–350 Cr cumulative, for total cumulative incremental investment of ~₹650–850 Cr. Against FY30 incremental Base revenue of ₹400 Cr, this works out to a 4-year cumulative R&D-to-revenue ratio in the 22–25% range — broadly in line with Freshworks (21% R&D-to-revenue, FY24)[19] and Intuit (19% R&D, FY24)[19]. Implied FY30 contribution margin lands in the 60–70% gross / 15–25% operating band, consistent with mid-cycle SaaS economics rather than a mature 30%+ operating margin.

Why not just 50 incremental hires?

The 74-FTE product build team alone cannot deliver ₹400+ Cr incremental revenue. Realistic delivery requires ~265 incremental FTEs at steady state across product, sales, success, channel, marketing, and regional ops — a ~5% addition to Tally's current ~4,950 base. Sales sized at one quota-carrying rep per ₹4–5 Cr ARR; success at one CSM per ₹6–8 Cr ARR; channel sized to retrain Tally's existing 28K reseller network for SaaS adjacency motion plus new bank/Big-4/ASP partnerships. The current section details only product builders (74 FTE); the broader org is referenced in the "Full incremental org" table above.

User-controllable assumptions

Run team as % of Build defaults to 70% with a 60–80% slider range[24]. Indian SaaS product teams typically retain 60–75% of Build headcount once a product reaches steady state. Cloud & inference cost loading defaults to Base, with Conservative and Aggressive options re-basing AI Copilot's cloud + LLM API lines[20][21]; other adjacencies move only marginally on this toggle.

What this model does NOT assume

No inorganic build or M&A; every FTE is hired in. No re-deployment from existing Tally org; every cost is incremental on top of the existing run rate. No revenue offset against Year-1 cost; this is a pure investment view, not NPV. No Year-2 productivity uplift from learning curve. The model is intentionally conservative on these four levers; relaxing any would reduce required investment.
